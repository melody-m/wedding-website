Travel and Parking

The Town Hall & The Bishop's Palace, Wells

Wells Town Hall and The Bishop's Palace and Gardens are next to each others. Wells is a lovely city but unfortunately one where it can be difficult to park especially on a Saturday as it's the market ! Most hotels have their own parking, so if you're staying overnight, we recommend walking to the venue.

Otherwise, there are a few long term car parks :

  • Waitrose, Whiting Way, Wells BA5 2PJ
  • Tucker Street Car Park, West St, Wells BA5 2HG

Questions

- Can I bring a +1?

Unfortunately no, we have a limited number so unless your partner is personally invited, we won't be able to accomodate them.

- Can I bring my children?

To give all our guests the opportunity to let their hair down and have a good time without having to worry about little fingers, eyes and ears we politely request no children. We hope that you understand and can still share our big day!

- When is the Rsvp deadline?

As we're having a small wedding, the sooner you can let us know, the easier it will be for us to organise. You can follow the link at the bottom of the page to rsvp online. We will send out formal invitations near the date with the Rsvp deadline which should be around two months before the wedding.

- At what time should I arrive?

If you are attending the whole day, the ceremony will start promptly at 2pm so make sure you have enough time to park and walk to the Town Hall. Saturdays are market days in Wells, so although it gives a very joyful atmosphere to the city, it is also busier and harder to make your way through.

- Where can I park?

Wells is a lovely historic city but pretty difficult to park in (especially on Saturdays) and unfortunately our venue doesn't provide any parking. For all of you that stay in nearby hotels, it would be preferable to let your car at the hotel's parking and just walk to the venue. Otherwise, we have put a map with the closest long term parking on the Details page. Wells is the smallest city of England, so don't worry, you won't have to walk for miles. However for anyone who might have difficulties walking, please let us know so that we can arrange a car to bring you through the market and drop you to the Town Hall. Both the Town Hall and The Bishop's Palace have lift access once inside.

- Where can I stay?

There are plenty of hotels and B&Bs in Wells but they need to be booked well in advance. We have put a few recommendations on the Details page as well.

- What should I wear? Do I need a hat?

We advise smart attire but hats are not required. However, if you feel like wearing one, you are welcome to!

- I have a special dietary requirement...

Our caterer is very flexible and considering we both have diet requirements, I'm sure they will be able to sort something out for you as long as you tell us in advance.

- What's the bar situation?

Prosecco, wine and soft drinks will be served at the reception, however if that's not really your cup of tea, you will be able purchase your own drink at the bar at the entrance of the Bishop's Palace.

- Any other questions?

If you have any other questions we haven't covered, please feel free to contact us.
